## Part B — Short answer

### 1. What is the difference between severity and priority?
Severity describes how badly a bug affects the system or business behavior. Priority describes how urgently the team wants to fix it.

### 2. Why can a bug be high priority but low severity?
Because even a small issue can affect a key flow, an important customer, or a public release. For example, a typo on the pricing page may be low severity technically but high priority for the business.

### 3. What makes a bug report useful for developers?
Clear title, environment, reproducible steps, actual result, expected result, evidence such as screenshots or logs, and enough detail to investigate quickly.

### 4. When would exploratory testing be especially valuable?
When requirements are unclear, the feature is new, there is limited documentation, or we want to uncover unexpected issues outside scripted test cases.

### 5. What should you test first in a login feature?
The most critical core flow: valid user logs in successfully. Then invalid password, empty fields, locked account, session behavior, error handling, and security-related checks.
